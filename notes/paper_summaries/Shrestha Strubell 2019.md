# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:**   
Emma Strubell, Ananya Ganesh, Andrew McCallum. *Proceedings of the 57th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL)*, 2019.  
https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/P19-1355

## Summary
This paper examines the financial and environmental costs of training modern deep learning models for natural language processing. The authors quantify the energy consumption and carbon emissions associated with training popular NLP models such as Transformer, ELMo, BERT, and GPT-2, showing that state-of-the-art accuracy often comes at a significant environmental cost. They demonstrate that model development and hyperparameter tuning can multiply these costs dramatically, sometimes equating to years of GPU usage. Based on these findings, the paper argues that unchecked growth in model size raises concerns about sustainability, cost, and accessibility in NLP research.

## Relevance to Our Survey
This paper is highly relevant to our survey because it provides concrete evidence of the sustainability trade-offs introduced by large AI models. Its analysis directly supports our discussion of the “energy-hungry AI” side of the sustainability paradox, complementing research that highlights AI’s ability to improve network efficiency. The policy and reporting recommendations proposed by the authors help frame why energy-aware AI design is essential when considering future wireless and networked systems.

## Critical Comment
One limitation of this work is that its energy and carbon estimates rely on approximations and hardware assumptions that may change as data center efficiency and renewable energy adoption improve. As a result, some numerical estimates may not fully reflect current or future deployment conditions, especially outside the United States.
