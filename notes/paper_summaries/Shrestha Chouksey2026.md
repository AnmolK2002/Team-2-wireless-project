# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:**  
Chouksey, A., Rajan, A. K., Gurjar, V., Tiwari, R., & Mishra, P. K. (2026). *The green paradox: The climate, environmental, and sustainability implications of artificial intelligence.* Global Environmental Change Advances, 6, 100029. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.gecadv.2025.100029

## Summary
This review examines the dual impact of artificial intelligence on environmental sustainability, highlighting the “AI sustainability paradox”: AI can drive environmental improvements while simultaneously increasing ecological strain. The authors discuss how large AI models and data centers demand massive energy, water, and hardware resources, creating high carbon emissions, e-waste, and resource depletion. Conversely, AI applications—such as climate modeling, renewable energy optimization, precision agriculture, and biodiversity monitoring—can support sustainable development and environmental management. The paper emphasizes the importance of low-carbon infrastructure, efficient model design, responsible hardware sourcing, and policy measures to mitigate AI’s negative impacts and promote long-term ecological viability.

## Relevance to Our Survey
This paper complements the project’s focus on the sustainability paradox by analyzing the environmental costs and benefits of AI beyond communication networks, highlighting systemic challenges such as energy use, scaling, and rebound effects. It provides broader context for understanding the environmental trade-offs of AI, supporting discussion on policy, infrastructure, and design strategies that can balance technological innovation with ecological responsibility.

## Critical Comment
While the review is comprehensive in scope, it largely synthesizes existing literature without offering new empirical data or quantitative comparison of AI’s net environmental impact across sectors. Future work could include lifecycle assessments or standardized metrics to better quantify the trade-offs and guide sustainable AI deployment.
