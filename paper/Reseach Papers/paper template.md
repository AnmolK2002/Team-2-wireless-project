

## Paper Summary

## Paper Title: Green, Quantized Federated Learning Over Wireless Networks: An Energy-Efficient Design
## Authors: Minsu Kim, Walid Saad, Mehdi Bennis, Mérouane Debbah
## Year: 2024
## Full Citation: Kim, M., Saad, W., Mozaffari, M., & Debbah, M. (2024). **Green, Quantized Federated Learning Over Wireless Networks: An Energy-Efficient Design**. IEEE Transactions on Wireless Communications, 23(2), 1386–1402.
## 1. Problem
The paper focuses on the problem of high energy costs due to communication overhead in federated learning over wireless networks. The large model update sizes in federated learning result in significant communication overhead, hence high energy costs.

## 2. Approach
The authors propose the use of quantized federated learning. In this approach, the model update sizes are reduced to minimize the number of bits being communicated. This ensures that model update sizes are reduced while maintaining accuracy.

## 3. Key Findings
The key findings in this paper are that the use of quantized federated learning results in reduced communication energy costs. This approach to federated learning is efficient in improving the performance of wireless learning with minimal performance degradation.

## 4. Relevance to Our Topic
The paper is relevant to our topic because it shows that AI systems can be made more energy efficient. This shows that AI development can be made green.
