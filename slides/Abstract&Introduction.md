Artificial intelligence (AI) is increasingly integrated into modern wireless networks to improve efficiency, enable automation, and support advanced 6G services. By intelligently managing network operations—such as dynamically controlling sleep states for cell towers and optimizing traffic flow—AI can reduce operational energy consumption and contribute to greener network infrastructure (Alhussien & Gulliver, 2024; Ezzeddine et al., 2024). However, the training, deployment, and continuous operation of large AI models require substantial computational resources, leading to high electricity and water consumption in data centers and contributing to a significant carbon footprint (Strubell, Ganesh, & McCallum, 2019; Kamran et al., 2025). This duality creates a sustainability paradox: AI can simultaneously enable energy-efficient networks and increase the environmental burden of network infrastructure. This paper surveys recent literature on AI-driven green networks and energy-aware 6G design, analyzes the trade-offs between network optimization and environmental costs, and proposes directions for balancing AI performance with sustainability to support future environmentally responsible wireless systems.



Introduction

The next generation of wireless networks, including 5G and the emerging 6G paradigm, promises unprecedented performance, low latency, and advanced connectivity services. Central to this evolution is the integration of artificial intelligence (AI), which allows networks to self-optimize, predict traffic patterns, and manage resources efficiently (Alhussien & Gulliver, 2024). Techniques such as dynamic sleep modes for cell towers, intelligent routing, and adaptive spectrum allocation demonstrate AI’s potential to reduce energy consumption and enable more sustainable network operations (Ezzeddine et al., 2024).

Despite these advantages, AI introduces new environmental challenges. Large-scale AI models rely on extensive data centers that require continuous operation, resulting in significant energy use, water consumption, and greenhouse gas emissions (Strubell, Ganesh, & McCallum, 2019). Consequently, the very technology that reduces operational energy can exacerbate environmental impacts elsewhere, creating what is termed the “sustainability paradox” (Safitra et al., 2023).

This paper examines this paradox by reviewing recent research on AI-enabled green networks, energy-efficient 5G and 6G infrastructure, and sustainable network design strategies. We aim to identify the trade-offs between AI-driven network optimization and environmental cost, highlight gaps in current research, and suggest potential frameworks for achieving a balance between AI efficiency and sustainability in future wireless networks (Alhussien & Gulliver, 2024; Ezzeddine et al., 2024; Kamran et al., 2025).
