## Proposed Idea / Future Direction

- Artificial intelligence (AI) has improved energy efficiency among wireless communication systems. The main techniques used include resource allocation, base control sleep schedules, and traffic prediction models. These approaches allow networks to adapt to conditions while reducing unnecessary energy consumption. Nevertheless, this type of technology does possess critical limitations. Limitations include training and deploying large-scale AI models requiring a large amount of computing power and carbon emissions to maximize performance. Therefore, creating a sustainability paradox, where AI does provide improvements, but it also increases environmental consequences. To address this situation, this paper suggests that an energy-awareness AI framework is needed for 6G networks. This framework would include minimizing efforts of running high-complex AI models and having models placed based on network conditions and energy limits. A hierarchy system would be introduced, where lightweight models are constantly monitoring and predicting. This would allow for computational power to be used selectively and be activated for advanced optimization.
